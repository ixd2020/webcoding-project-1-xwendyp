Highlight

John La farge has done a lot of artworks. While he is famous for his stained glass works, I would like to share more of his other works, such as watercolour and painting, and these are some of the highlights that I recommend checking it out.

Portrait of the Painter

This self-portrait, painted by La Farge at his family's estate in Glen Cove, Long Island, in October 1859, illustrates the use of a picture as a picturesque source showing the pleasure of the unmodulated forms, flattened space of Japanese prints and his reliance on broad tonal masses in the manner of his teachers, Thomas Couture and William Morris Hunt.

Date: 1859
Medium: Oil on wood panel
Classification: Paintings

Nocturne

La Farge used the medium to research, document his travels and paint exquisite floral still-life exhibition pieces in his illustrations and decorative projects. It typifies those still lifes in its quiet mood and gives echoes of Japanese asymmetrical composition and large tonal planes.

Date: CA. 1885
Medium: Watercolor, gouache, and charcoal on off-white wove paper adhered to wove paper
Classification: Drawings

The Great Statue of Amida Buddha at Kamakura, Known as the Daibutsu, from the Priest's Garden

La Farge uses watercolour to create studies for illustrations and to paint sheets for exhibition on a trip to Japan with Henry Adams in 1886. Upon his return to New York, he executed this bold and monumental composition, using watercolour sketch made during his journeys, as well as the photographs he and Adams had taken. The Daibutsu, or Great Buddha, a fifty-foot-high bronze cast in 1252, is known for its massive height, its peaceful demeanour, and its unique open-air environment surrounded by mountains and trees.

Date: 1887
Medium: Watercolor and gouache on off-white wove paper
Classification: Drawings
